Attitude Dynamics

Euler's equation is defined as:

M⃗=Jω⃗˙+ω⃗×Jω⃗ \vec M = \mathbf{J} \dot{\vec \omega} + \vec \omega^\times \mathbf{J} \vec \omega M=Jω˙+ω×Jω

Assuming principal axis rotations, the equation can be expanded to:

Mx=Jxω˙x−(Jy−Jz)ωyωzMy=Jyω˙y−(Jz−Jx)ωxωzMz=Jzω˙z−(Jx−Jy)ωxωy \begin{aligned} M_x &= J_x \dot \omega_x - (J_y - J_z) \omega_y \omega_z \\ M_y &= J_y \dot \omega_y - (J_z - J_x) \omega_x \omega_z \\ M_z &= J_z \dot \omega_z - (J_x - J_y) \omega_x \omega_y \end{aligned} Mx​My​Mz​​=Jx​ω˙x​−(Jy​−Jz​)ωy​ωz​=Jy​ω˙y​−(Jz​−Jx​)ωx​ωz​=Jz​ω˙z​−(Jx​−Jy​)ωx​ωy​​

Attitude Control

PD Control

Frequency domain definition:

Mc=(Kp+sKd)Δθ~HC=Kp+sKd \begin{aligned} M_c &= (K_p + sK_d) \tilde{\Delta \theta} \\ H_C &= K_p + sK_d \end{aligned} Mc​HC​​=(Kp​+sKd​)Δθ~=Kp​+sKd​​

Time-domain definition:

Mc=KpΔθ+KdΔθ˙=Kp(θref−θ)+Kd(θ˙ref−θ˙) M_c = K_p \Delta \theta + K_d \Delta \dot \theta = K_p (\theta_\text{ref} - \theta) + K_d (\dot \theta_\text{ref} - \dot \theta) Mc​=Kp​Δθ+Kd​Δθ˙=Kp​(θref​−θ)+Kd​(θ˙ref​−θ˙)

Defining gains in terms of natural frequency and damping ratio:

Kp=Jωn2 K_p = J \omega_n^2 Kp​=Jωn2​ Kd=2Jωnζ K_d = 2 J \omega_n \zeta Kd​=2Jωn​ζ

Quaternion Feedback Control

Control law:

u⃗=−Kq⃗err,v−Cω⃗err \vec u = - \mathbf K \vec q_{\text{err}, v} - \mathbf C \vec \omega_\text{err} u=−Kq​err,v​−Cωerr​

Known formulations:

K=KI, C=diag(C1,C2,C3), K,Ci>0 \mathbf K = K \mathbf I, \; \mathbf C = \text{diag}(C_1, C_2, C_3), \; K, C_i > 0 K=KI,C=diag(C1​,C2​,C3​),K,Ci​>0 K=Kqerr,s3I, C=diag(C1,C2,C3) \mathbf K = \frac{K}{q_{\text{err}, s}^3} \mathbf I, \; \mathbf C = \text{diag}(C_1, C_2, C_3) K=qerr,s3​K​I,C=diag(C1​,C2​,C3​) K=Ksign(qerr,s)I, C=diag(C1,C2,C3) \mathbf K = K \text{sign}(q_{\text{err}, s}) \mathbf I, \; \mathbf C = \text{diag}(C_1, C_2, C_3) K=Ksign(qerr,s​)I,C=diag(C1​,C2​,C3​) K=[αJ+βI]−1, K−1C≤0, α,β≤0 \mathbf K = \left[ \alpha \mathbf J + \beta \mathbf I \right]^{-1}, \; \mathbf K^{-1} \mathbf C \leq 0, \; \alpha, \beta \leq 0 K=[αJ+βI]−1,K−1C≤0,α,β≤0
